Overview

The events collectively referred to as "Before Tekken" encompass the history of the Mishima family and the origins of the Devil Gene prior to the First King of Iron Fist Tournament. Although these events occur before the beginning of the playable story, they form the foundation of nearly every major conflict in the Tekken series.

At the center of this history stands the Mishima family, a bloodline whose internal struggles would shape the political, economic, and military landscape of the world. Through the Mishima Zaibatsu, the family accumulated extraordinary wealth and influence, eventually becoming one of the most powerful forces on Earth. Yet despite its success, the Mishima family became defined not by unity, but by betrayal, ambition, and revenge.

The story begins with Jinpachi Mishima, founder of the Mishima Zaibatsu and creator of Mishima Style Fighting Karate. Under Jinpachi's leadership, the organization prospered and established itself as a respected corporation. However, the future of the family changed dramatically when Jinpachi's son, Heihachi Mishima, seized control of the Zaibatsu through force. This act not only transformed the organization but also marked the beginning of the cycle of violence that would plague the Mishima bloodline for generations.

The situation became even more complicated through Heihachi's marriage to Kazumi Hachijo. Unknown to most of the world, Kazumi belonged to the Hachijo Clan, an ancient bloodline connected to the mysterious power known as the Devil Gene. Her relationship with Heihachi brought this supernatural force into the Mishima family and forever altered its destiny.

The birth of Kazuya Mishima united two powerful bloodlines. As the son of Heihachi Mishima and Kazumi Hachijo, Kazuya stood at the crossroads of the family's future. What should have been the beginning of a new generation instead became the catalyst for tragedy.

The conflict reached its first major turning point when Kazumi confronted Heihachi. Believing him to be a threat to the world, she attempted to kill him. Their battle ended with Kazumi's death at Heihachi's hands. In the aftermath, Heihachi became increasingly suspicious that his son had inherited the same supernatural power that had manifested within Kazumi.

This suspicion led to one of the most infamous events in Tekken history: the Cliff Incident. Heihachi threw the young Kazuya into a deep ravine. According to early versions of the story, this was intended as a brutal test of strength. Later games, particularly Tekken 7, presented an alternative explanation in which Heihachi sought proof that Kazuya possessed the Devil Gene. Regardless of the motive, the consequences were the same. Kazuya survived, awakened the Devil Gene, and developed a hatred for his father that would define the rest of his life.

The events of the Before Tekken era established the central themes of the franchise: family conflict, the corrupting influence of power, and the consequences of revenge. Every major storyline that follows—from Kazuya's rebellion against Heihachi to Jin Kazama's struggle with the Devil Gene—can be traced back to the decisions made during this period.

Jinpachi Mishima

Jinpachi Mishima was the founder of the Mishima Zaibatsu and the patriarch of the Mishima family. Long before the events of the Tekken series, he established both the organization and the martial arts tradition that would later become known as Mishima Style Fighting Karate. Although the Mishima name would eventually become associated with warfare, corruption, and family betrayal, Jinpachi himself is generally remembered as one of the most honorable members of the bloodline.

Under Jinpachi's leadership, the Mishima Zaibatsu grew into a powerful and influential corporation. While the exact nature of the organization's early activities has never been fully detailed, it was considerably less aggressive than it would become under later leadership. Jinpachi valued discipline, personal strength, and martial arts philosophy, believing that power carried responsibility. These principles would ultimately place him at odds with his son.

Jinpachi spent much of his life refining and teaching Mishima Style Fighting Karate. The style emphasized overwhelming offensive pressure, powerful strikes, and absolute confidence in battle. Over time it became one of the most feared martial arts disciplines in the world. Many of the techniques used by Heihachi, Kazuya, and Jin can be traced back to the foundations established by Jinpachi.

Despite his accomplishments, Jinpachi's greatest failure may have been his relationship with his son, Heihachi Mishima. As Heihachi matured, he became increasingly ambitious and began to view his father's leadership as weak. Where Jinpachi sought stability and discipline, Heihachi sought expansion and absolute control.

Eventually Heihachi launched a coup and seized control of the Mishima Zaibatsu. Jinpachi was overthrown and imprisoned beneath Hon-Maru Temple. The outside world believed he had died, but in reality he remained trapped underground for decades. During this period the Zaibatsu transformed dramatically under Heihachi's leadership, becoming far more ruthless and militarized.

While imprisoned, Jinpachi's body weakened and eventually died. However, his story did not end there. At some point after his death, a malevolent supernatural entity took possession of his corpse. The being (later often referred to by fans as a devil-like spirit, though Namco has never fully explained what is was) revived Jinpachi and granted him immense power, though it also slowly consumed his mind and body. This possessed version of Jinpachi would later emerge during the events of Tekken 5.

Even after his death and resurrection, Jinpachi remained fundamentally different from the other Mishimas. Unlike Heihachi, Kazuya, or Jin, he did not seek domination or revenge. In many ways he serves as a reminder of what the Mishima family might have become had its future not been defined by betrayal and hatred.

The Rise of Heihachi Mishima

From a young age, Heihachi Mishima displayed an ambition and ruthlessness that set him apart from his father. While Jinpachi valued honor, discipline, and stability, Heihachi believed that true leadership belonged to the strong. He viewed weakness as a flaw that invited failure and saw power as something to be seized rather than inherited.

As the years passed, Heihachi became increasingly dissatisfied with the direction of the Mishima Zaibatsu. Although the organization was successful under Jinpachi's leadership, Heihachi believed it lacked the strength and aggression necessary to reach its full potential. This difference in philosophy created a growing divide between father and son.

Eventually, Heihachi acted on his ambitions. He launched a coup against Jinpachi and seized control of the Mishima Zaibatsu by force. The exact details of the takeover remain unclear, but its outcome reshaped the future of the Mishima family forever. Jinpachi was imprisoned beneath Hon-Maru Temple, where he was left to die, while Heihachi assumed leadership of the organization.

Under Heihachi's rule, the Mishima Zaibatsu changed dramatically. What had once been a powerful corporation became an increasingly militarized global force. Heihachi expanded its influence across numerous industries and transformed it into one of the most powerful organizations in the world. Over time, the Zaibatsu developed private military capabilities and became capable of influencing international affairs on a massive scale.

Heihachi's rise to power established a pattern that would define the Mishima bloodline for generations. Family bonds became secondary to strength, and betrayal became a recurring feature of the family's history. The overthrow of Jinpachi was the first major fracture within the Mishima family, but it would not be the last.

Despite his ruthless methods, Heihachi genuinely believed that strength was necessary for survival. Throughout his life he would repeatedly test those closest to him, often through brutal and seemingly cruel means. In his mind, these actions separated the strong from the weak and ensured that only the worthy would endure.

By the time the events involving Kazumi Hachijo and the Devil Gene unfolded, Heihachi had already become one of the most powerful and feared individuals in the world. His decisions during this period would not only determine the future of the Mishima Zaibatsu, but also the fate of his family for generations to come.

Kazumi Hachijo

Kazumi Hachijo was the wife of Heihachi Mishima, the mother of Kazuya Mishima, and a member of the mysterious Hachijo Clan. Although she was introduced relatively late in the series, her existence fundamentally changed the understanding of the Mishima family's history and the origins of the Devil Gene.

The Hachijo Clan was an ancient bloodline connected to the Devil Gene. For generations, members of the clan monitored individuals they believed could become threats to the world. According to the account presented in Tekken 7, the clan eventually identified Heihachi Mishima as one such threat due to his growing ambition and increasing accumulation of power.

At some point during her youth, Kazumi met Heihachi. Despite the mission entrusted to her by the Hachijo Clan, the two developed a genuine relationship and eventually married. Their union appeared successful, and together they had a son, Kazuya Mishima. For a time, the family lived peacefully, and Kazumi seemed to abandon the role she had originally been expected to fulfill.

However, as Heihachi's power continued to grow following his takeover of the Mishima Zaibatsu, Kazumi became increasingly concerned about the man he was becoming. According to her own statements, she had been instructed that if Heihachi ever became a threat to the world, she would have to stop him, regardless of her personal feelings.

This conflict between duty and love defined much of Kazumi's life. Unlike many characters in the series, she was not motivated by greed, conquest, or revenge. Instead, she found herself trapped between her loyalty to her family and her responsibility to the Hachijo Clan. Both choices carried devastating consequences.

Eventually, Kazumi concluded that Heihachi had become too dangerous to ignore. She revealed her connection to the Devil Gene and transformed into her Devil form. The revelation shocked Heihachi, who had been completely unaware of the supernatural power hidden within his wife.

The battle that followed became one of the most significant events in Tekken history. Despite Kazumi's immense power, Heihachi ultimately emerged victorious and killed her. At some point before the main events of the series, Kazumi entrusted Akuma with a solemn request: if either Heihachi or Kazuya ever became a threat to the world, he was to kill them. Years later, during the events of Tekken 7, Akuma fulfilled that promise by seeking out both father and son in an attempt to carry out Kazumi's final wish.

Although Kazumi died before the main events of the series began, her influence continued to shape the story long after her death. Through Kazuya, the Devil Gene became part of the Mishima bloodline. Her final actions set in motion the events that would eventually lead to the conflicts between Heihachi, Kazuya, and Jin Kazama.

In many ways, Kazumi serves as the catalyst for the entire Tekken saga. Her marriage united the Mishima and Hachijo bloodlines, her death fueled Heihachi's fear of the Devil Gene, and her legacy continued through every generation that followed.

The Devil Gene

The Devil Gene is one of the most mysterious and influential forces in the Tekken universe. Although it is often referred to as a "gene," its exact nature remains unclear. Throughout the series it has been described as a hereditary power passed through bloodlines, yet its abilities and supernatural properties suggest that it is far more than a simple genetic trait.

The Devil Gene grants its hosts extraordinary abilities far beyond those of ordinary humans. Known powers include immense physical strength, enhanced speed and durability, accelerated healing, energy projection, levitation or flight, and the ability to transform into powerful Devil forms. These transformations often alter the user's appearance, granting wings, horns, glowing eyes, and other demonic characteristics.

The first known carrier within the main storyline was Kazumi Hachijo, a member of the Hachijo Clan. Through her marriage to Heihachi Mishima, the Devil Gene entered the Mishima bloodline and was inherited by their son, Kazuya Mishima. Following the Cliff Incident, Kazuya's Devil Gene awakened, allowing him to survive a fall that should have killed him and beginning his transformation into one of the most dangerous individuals in the world.

For many years, the nature of Kazuya's power was poorly understood. Earlier entries in the series often implied that Kazuya had formed a pact with a supernatural devil after being thrown from the cliff. However, later games reinterpreted this concept and established that the power originated from the Devil Gene itself. As a result, many early references to a separate "Devil" entity have since been folded into the broader mythology surrounding the gene.

The Devil Gene later manifested in Jin Kazama, the son of Kazuya Mishima and Jun Kazama. Unlike Kazuya, Jin viewed the power as a curse and spent much of his life searching for a way to free himself from its influence. His struggle with the Devil Gene became one of the central conflicts of the series and helped distinguish him from his father.

Although the Devil Gene can grant incredible power, it is often associated with emotional instability, destructive impulses, and a heightened capacity for violence. Characters who possess it frequently struggle with questions of identity, self-control, and destiny. Whether these effects are caused directly by the gene or by the circumstances surrounding its inheritance remains a matter of debate.

During the events of Tekken 6, the Devil Gene's origins became more closely connected to Azazel, an ancient supernatural being said to have existed for thousands of years. According to the lore introduced in that game, Azazel may be linked to the source of the Devil Gene itself, though the exact relationship has never been fully explained. This revelation expanded the scope of the story beyond the Mishima family and connected their struggle to a much older supernatural conflict.

Despite decades of lore, many questions about the Devil Gene remain unanswered. Its precise origins, its full capabilities, and the reason it manifests differently in various hosts continue to be subjects of speculation among both characters and fans. As a result, the Devil Gene remains one of the greatest mysteries in Tekken history.

The Death of Kazumi

As Heihachi's power continued to grow, Kazumi became increasingly convinced that her husband was becoming the very threat the Hachijo Clan had feared. Torn between her love for her family and her duty to her bloodline, she found herself facing an impossible choice.

According to the account presented in Tekken 7, Kazumi had been raised with a specific mission. If Heihachi Mishima ever became a danger to the world, she was to stop him, even if it meant sacrificing her own happiness. For years she attempted to balance this responsibility with her desire to live a normal life alongside Heihachi and Kazuya.

Eventually, Kazumi concluded that Heihachi's ambitions could no longer be ignored. The man she had once loved had transformed the Mishima Zaibatsu into an increasingly powerful and militarized organization, and she feared what he might become if left unchecked.

When the time came, Kazumi revealed the secret she had hidden throughout their marriage. She possessed the Devil Gene and could transform into a powerful Devil form. For Heihachi, the revelation was shocking. Not only had his wife concealed her true nature from him, but she now stood before him as an enemy.

The battle between Heihachi and Kazumi was both a personal tragedy and a turning point in history. Husband and wife fought not out of hatred, but because each believed they were protecting the future in their own way. Kazumi believed she was preventing a catastrophe. Heihachi believed he was defending himself and his family from a supernatural threat.

Despite the immense power granted by the Devil Gene, Kazumi was ultimately defeated. Heihachi emerged victorious and killed his wife. Her death marked the end of their family and left a lasting scar on both Heihachi and the future of the Mishima bloodline.

Before the main events of the series, Kazumi entrusted Akuma with a solemn request. If either Heihachi or Kazuya ever became a threat to the world, he was to kill them. Years later, Akuma would attempt to fulfill that promise during the events of Tekken 7.

Although Kazumi died before the beginning of the series, her influence continued to shape every major conflict that followed. Her death intensified Heihachi's fear of the Devil Gene, contributed to the events surrounding Kazuya's childhood, and ensured that the Hachijo bloodline would remain intertwined with the fate of the Mishima family.

The Cliff Incident

The Cliff Incident is one of the most infamous and consequential events in Tekken history. Although it occurred years before the First King of Iron Fist Tournament, its effects would shape the lives of Heihachi Mishima, Kazuya Mishima, Jin Kazama, and countless others for generations.

Following the death of Kazumi Hachijo, Heihachi became increasingly concerned that his son had inherited the same supernatural power that had manifested within his wife. Kazuya was still a child at the time and had little understanding of the conflict that had torn his family apart. Nevertheless, he would soon become the focus of his father's suspicions.

One day, Heihachi took the young Kazuya to the edge of a steep cliff. Without warning, he threw his son into the ravine below. The fall should have been fatal. Instead, Kazuya survived.

For many years, the reason behind Heihachi's actions appeared straightforward. Early entries in the series described the event as a brutal test of strength. According to this version of the story, Heihachi believed that only the strong deserved to carry the Mishima name. If Kazuya was incapable of surviving the fall, then he was unworthy of being Heihachi's son. This explanation aligned closely with Heihachi's long-established philosophy that strength was the only quality that truly mattered.

However, Tekken 7 introduced a different interpretation. During his account of the family's history, Heihachi claimed that he threw Kazuya from the cliff in order to determine whether his son possessed the Devil Gene. Having witnessed Kazumi's transformation and fought her Devil form firsthand, Heihachi feared that the same power might exist within Kazuya. According to this explanation, the fall was intended to reveal the truth. If Kazuya survived, it would prove that he carried the Devil Gene. If he died, then Heihachi's fears would be proven unfounded.

This newer explanation remains a subject of debate among fans. One reason for the controversy is that the story comes primarily from Heihachi himself, a character whose version of events cannot always be accepted without question. Some fans believe the original explanation and the Tekken 7 explanation are not mutually exclusive. In this interpretation, Heihachi may have been motivated by both his obsession with strength and his fear of the Devil Gene.

Regardless of his motives, the consequences were catastrophic. As Kazuya fell, the Devil Gene awakened within him. The power allowed him to survive an ordeal that should have killed him. More importantly, the experience permanently altered his relationship with his father.

For Kazuya, the Cliff Incident was not simply an act of cruelty. It was the moment he learned that the person who should have protected him was willing to throw him away. The betrayal left deep psychological scars and fueled a hatred that would define much of his life. Even decades later, Kazuya's actions would continue to be influenced by the resentment born on that cliff.

The incident also marked the beginning of the Devil Gene's active role within the Mishima bloodline. Although the power had previously existed within Kazumi, Kazuya's survival demonstrated that it had successfully passed to a new generation. From that moment onward, the conflict between the Mishima family and the Devil Gene became inseparable.

In many ways, the Cliff Incident serves as the true beginning of the Tekken saga. Without it, Kazuya might never have awakened the Devil Gene. Without the Devil Gene, the rivalry between Heihachi and Kazuya would likely have taken a very different form. Nearly every major event in the series can be traced back to the consequences of that single moment.

More than any tournament, battle, or global conflict, the Cliff Incident represents the central theme of Tekken: the destructive cycle of family betrayal. What began as a father's decision on a remote cliff would eventually grow into a struggle that affected the entire world.

Legacy of the Before Tekken Era

By the end of the Before Tekken era, the foundations of the entire Tekken saga had been established. The Mishima Zaibatsu had fallen under Heihachi Mishima's control, the Devil Gene had entered the Mishima bloodline through Kazumi Hachijo, and the relationship between Heihachi and Kazuya had been irreparably damaged.

The death of Kazumi and the Cliff Incident left lasting scars on both father and son. Heihachi became increasingly convinced that the Devil Gene represented a dangerous threat, while Kazuya grew to despise the man who had betrayed him. Their conflict would continue to intensify as Kazuya matured, eventually developing into one of the most famous rivalries in video game history.

As the years passed, Heihachi continued to expand the Mishima Zaibatsu's influence and power. Under his leadership, the organization became one of the most dominant forces in the world. However, his position was far from secure. The hatred growing within Kazuya ensured that the struggle for control of the Mishima family was far from over.

At the same time, the Devil Gene remained hidden within Kazuya. Although its existence was not widely known, the power continued to shape his destiny and would eventually play a major role in the future of the Mishima bloodline.

These tensions ultimately culminated in the announcement of the First King of Iron Fist Tournament. Publicly, the tournament appeared to be a showcase of martial arts excellence sponsored by the Mishima Zaibatsu. In reality, it would become the stage upon which years of resentment, ambition, and family conflict would finally erupt.

With the beginning of the First King of Iron Fist Tournament, the events of the Before Tekken era came to an end and the story of Tekken 1 began.
